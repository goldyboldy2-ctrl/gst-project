What Exactly is GST?

GST stands for Goods and Services Tax. It is an indirect tax that is charged on almost every product and service sold in India ‚Äî from your morning milk to the software your company uses.

Before GST (launched on July 1, 2017), India had a complicated maze of taxes like VAT, Service Tax, Excise Duty, and many others. Imagine paying 5 different tolls on the same highway. GST replaced all of them with one single, streamlined tax.

How Does GST Work?

GST is charged at every stage of the supply chain ‚Äî from manufacturing to the final sale to you. The key idea is called "Input Tax Credit". Here's how it works simply:

üì¶ Real-Life Example: A Shirt Journey

Step 1: A cotton farmer sells cotton to a factory. Factory pays GST on cotton.

Step 2: The factory makes shirts and sells to a wholesaler. Wholesaler pays GST ‚Äî but the factory gets back the GST it paid on cotton (Input Tax Credit).

Step 3: The wholesaler sells to a shop. Shop pays GST ‚Äî but gets back what it paid to the wholesaler.

Step 4: You buy the shirt. You pay GST only once ‚Äî on the final price.

üéØ Result: Tax is collected only on the value added at each stage. No double taxation!

What Are the GST Rates?

India has a tiered GST structure. Not everything is taxed at the same rate:

GST Rate What Falls Here
0% (Exempt) Milk, fresh fruits, vegetables, basic foodgrains, healthcare services
5% Rice, wheat, edible oils, branded snacks, public transport
12% Smartphones, textiles, certain medicines, restaurant meals (non-AC)
18% AC restaurants, electronics, most services, business software
28% Cars, tobacco, luxury goods, casinos

What is CGST, SGST, and IGST?

You might see these on your invoices. Here's the simple breakdown:

  • CGST (Central GST) ‚Äî Goes to the Central Government. Charged on sales within the same state.
  • SGST (State GST) ‚Äî Goes to the State Government. Also charged on sales within the same state.
  • IGST (Integrated GST) ‚Äî Charged when you sell something from one state to another. It combines CGST + SGST into one tax.

üìç Quick Example

You buy a laptop in Maharashtra (same state sale) at 18% GST ‚Üí You pay 9% CGST + 9% SGST

You buy a laptop shipped from Delhi to Maharashtra (inter-state) at 18% GST ‚Üí You pay 18% IGST

Who Needs to Register for GST?

Not everyone needs a GST number. You must register if:

  • Your annual turnover exceeds ‚Çπ40 lakhs (goods) or ‚Çπ20 lakhs (services)
  • You sell goods across states (inter-state supply)
  • You sell on e-commerce platforms like Amazon or Flipkart
  • You are a casual taxable person or import services

Why Does GST Matter for You?

Even if you are not a business owner, GST affects your daily life. It determines the price you pay for groceries, electricity, restaurants, and services. Understanding GST helps you:

  • Know why prices are what they are
  • Claim refunds if you are a business owner
  • Avoid penalties and stay compliant
  • Make informed decisions when starting a business
