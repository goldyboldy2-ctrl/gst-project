Major GST Amendments in 2026

The GST Council's latest meetings have introduced several significant changes affecting businesses across India. Here's what you need to know:

Action Required

Many of these changes are already in effect or will be implemented in phases throughout 2026. Review your compliance processes immediately to avoid penalties.

1. E-Invoicing Threshold Reduction

Effective Date: 1st April 2026

What Changed

Old Rule: E-invoicing mandatory for businesses with turnover > ‚Çπ10 crore

New Rule: E-invoicing mandatory for businesses with turnover > ‚Çπ5 crore

Impact: Approximately 2 lakh additional businesses now required to implement e-invoicing

Who's Affected

  • Businesses with annual turnover between ‚Çπ5-10 crore
  • B2B suppliers and manufacturers in this bracket
  • Service providers exceeding ‚Çπ5 crore turnover

Action Steps

  • Register on the e-invoice portal (IRP)
  • Update your accounting/billing software
  • Train staff on e-invoice generation
  • Test the system before April 1st

2. GSTR-2B Auto-Population Enhancement

Effective Date: Already implemented (January 2026)

What Changed

GSTR-2B now includes additional data points:

  • Amendment details from previous periods
  • Credit/debit notes issued by suppliers
  • TCS (Tax Collected at Source) details
  • Import of services details
  • Revised ISD credit distribution

Impact on Businesses

  • Better ITC reconciliation: More comprehensive view of available credits
  • Reduced manual work: Automatic population of most entries
  • Higher accuracy: Less scope for manual errors
  • Stricter compliance: Easier for department to spot discrepancies

3. Late Fee Amnesty Scheme

Window: 1st March 2026 - 31st May 2026

One-Time Relief

Government has announced amnesty for delayed return filing:

  • For FY 2020-21 to 2024-25: Maximum late fee capped at ‚Çπ500 per return (‚Çπ250 CGST + ‚Çπ250 SGST)
  • Nil returns: No late fee if filed during amnesty period
  • Condition: Must file all pending returns by 31st May 2026

Potential savings: Up to ‚Çπ10,000 per return waived!

Don't Miss This Opportunity

If you have pending returns from previous years, file them before 31st May 2026 to save thousands in late fees. This is a limited-time window.

5. Composition Scheme Turnover Limit Increase

Effective Date: 1st April 2026

What Changed

Old Limit: ‚Çπ1.5 crore annual turnover

New Limit: ‚Çπ2 crore annual turnover

Benefit: More small businesses can opt for simplified compliance

Who Benefits

  • Small traders with turnover between ‚Çπ1.5-2 crore
  • Restaurants not serving alcohol
  • Small manufacturers
  • Service providers (up to ‚Çπ75 lakh turnover limit remains same)

6. Biometric Authentication for High-Risk Taxpayers

Phase 1 Rollout: March 2026

New Security Measure

Selected taxpayers flagged as high-risk will need to complete biometric authentication (Aadhaar-based) for:

  • Filing returns
  • Claiming refunds
  • Making amendments to registration
  • Cancelling registration

Risk Factors

Taxpayers may be flagged if they have:

  • Multiple registration cancellations
  • History of fake invoicing
  • Irregular return filing patterns
  • Disproportionate ITC claims
  • Non-existent business address

Maintain Clean Record

File returns on time, maintain proper documentation, and ensure your business address is valid to avoid being flagged for additional verification.

7. GSTR-1 Amendment Window Closure

Effective Date: Already implemented (January 2026)

What Changed

Old Rule: Could amend GSTR-1 of previous months anytime before filing annual return

New Rule: GSTR-1 can only be amended up to the due date of filing GSTR-1 for September of next financial year

Example: For FY 2025-26, all amendments must be done by October 11, 2026

Impact

  • Stricter timeline for corrections
  • Need to reconcile and amend earlier
  • Cannot wait until annual return filing
  • Better data hygiene required

8. TCS Rate Increase for E-Commerce Operators

Effective Date: 1st April 2026

What Changed

Old Rate: 1% TCS (0.5% CGST + 0.5% SGST/UTGST)

New Rate: 2% TCS (1% CGST + 1% SGST/UTGST)

Applies to: All supplies through e-commerce operators (Amazon, Flipkart, etc.)

What This Means

  • For sellers: More tax deducted from payments received
  • For e-commerce operators: Must collect and remit higher TCS
  • Cash flow impact: Sellers will receive less upfront, claim as credit later
  • Reconciliation: More important to match TCS with GSTR-2B

9. HSN Code Mandate Extension

Effective Date: 1st April 2026

Turnover Bracket Old Requirement New Requirement
Up to ‚Çπ5 crore 4-digit HSN 6-digit HSN
‚Çπ5 crore to ‚Çπ100 crore 6-digit HSN 8-digit HSN
Above ‚Çπ100 crore 8-digit HSN 8-digit HSN (no change)

Action Required

Update your product master with correct 6-digit or 8-digit HSN codes. Incorrect HSN codes can lead to GSTR-1 rejection and compliance issues.

10. Quarterly ITC Reconciliation Mandatory

Effective Date: FY 2026-27 onwards

New Compliance Requirement

Starting FY 2026-27, businesses must file a quarterly ITC reconciliation statement:

  • Form: New GSTR-2C (different from auto-generated GSTR-2B)
  • Due Date: 25th of month following quarter end
  • Purpose: Match ITC claimed in GSTR-3B with GSTR-2B
  • Penalty: ‚Çπ200 per day for late filing (max ‚Çπ5,000)

Compliance Checklist for 2026

Immediate Actions (By March 2026)

  • Check if you fall under new e-invoicing threshold (‚Çπ5 crore)
  • File all pending returns to avail amnesty scheme
  • Review and update HSN codes in product master
  • Update GST rate changes in billing software
  • Verify if composition scheme limit increase applies to you

By April 2026

  • Implement e-invoicing if newly covered
  • Adjust TCS deduction rates if selling on e-commerce
  • Start using enhanced GSTR-2B features
  • Ensure 6/8-digit HSN codes on all invoices

Ongoing (2026 onwards)

  • File quarterly ITC reconciliation starting FY 2026-27
  • Maintain biometric authentication readiness if flagged
  • Complete GSTR-1 amendments within new timeline
  • Monitor GST Council meetings for further updates
