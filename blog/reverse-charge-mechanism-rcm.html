Reverse Charge Mechanism (RCM) in GST: Complete Guide 2026

‚è±Ô∏è 14 min read üìÖ Updated: February 11, 2026

Quick Summary: Reverse Charge Mechanism (RCM) is when the recipient (buyer) pays GST instead of the supplier. Applies to specific goods/services, unregistered suppliers, and import of services. Recipient cannot use input tax credit from cash ledger.

What is Reverse Charge Mechanism (RCM)?

```

Under normal GST, the supplier charges GST and pays it to the government. Under Reverse Charge Mechanism (RCM), the liability to pay GST shifts from the supplier to the recipient (buyer).

Normal GST (Forward Charge)

Supplier: Charges GST on invoice

Supplier: Pays GST to government

Recipient: Pays GST to supplier

Recipient: Claims ITC

Reverse Charge (RCM)

Supplier: Does NOT charge GST

Supplier: No GST payment obligation

Recipient: Pays GST directly to government

Recipient: Can claim ITC (with conditions)

Legal Basis

  • Section 9(3) & 9(4): RCM on specified goods and services
  • Section 5(3) & 5(4): RCM on import of services
  • Notification 13/2017: List of RCM goods/services

Why RCM Exists

  • ‚úÖ Prevent tax evasion by unregistered suppliers
  • ‚úÖ Ensure GST compliance in sensitive sectors
  • ‚úÖ Simplify taxation for small suppliers
  • ‚úÖ Control tax collection in import scenarios
```

When Does Reverse Charge Apply?

```

Three Main Scenarios

1

Notified Goods & Services

Specific goods and services listed by government where RCM always applies

Example: Legal services, GTA services, supply of tobacco

2

Unregistered Supplier to Registered Recipient

When unregistered person supplies to GST-registered person

Status: Currently suspended for most goods/services

3

Import of Services

When you import services from outside India

Example: Software subscription from US company

RCM for Unregistered Suppliers (Important Update)

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: RCM on purchases from unregistered suppliers (Section 9(4)) has been suspended indefinitely since 2017. It only applies to notified categories and import of services.

This means: If you buy from an unregistered local supplier (except for notified goods/services), you don't need to pay RCM.

```

```
E-commerce Services Under RCM

E-commerce operators (like Amazon, Flipkart) must pay GST under RCM for:

  • Restaurant services provided through platform
  • Accommodation services (hotels, homestays) through platform
  • Housekeeping services through platform
  • Transportation of passengers via platform
```

How to Pay GST Under Reverse Charge

```

Step-by-Step Payment Process

1

Receive Invoice Without GST

Supplier issues invoice mentioning "Supply under Reverse Charge" but doesn't add GST

2

Calculate GST Payable

Calculate applicable GST on the invoice value

Example: ‚Çπ10,000 service √ó 18% = ‚Çπ1,800 GST

3

Pay From Cash Ledger

You MUST pay RCM from cash ledger (electronic cash ledger)

ITC credit CANNOT be used for RCM payment

4

Declare in GSTR-3B

Report RCM liability in Table 3.1(d) and Table 4(A)(3) of GSTR-3B

5

Claim ITC (if eligible)

In the same month, claim ITC in Table 4(A)(3) of GSTR-3B

6

Report in GSTR-1

Not required for most RCM transactions. Only for specific scenarios.

GSTR-3B Reporting

Table What to Report
Table 3.1(d) Inward supplies on which tax is payable under RCM
Table 4(A)(3) ITC available on RCM (same amount as Table 3.1(d))
Table 6.1 Net payment after ITC adjustment (usually becomes zero)

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical: You cannot pay RCM from ITC credit ledger. You must deposit cash first, then claim ITC. This is called "pay and claim" mechanism.
```

Input Tax Credit (ITC) Rules Under RCM

```

Can You Claim ITC on RCM?

Yes, you can claim ITC on GST paid under RCM, subject to normal ITC conditions:

  • ‚úÖ You are registered under GST
  • ‚úÖ Goods/services used for business purposes
  • ‚úÖ You have valid invoice/document
  • ‚úÖ You have actually paid the GST under RCM
  • ‚úÖ You have filed GSTR-3B declaring the RCM liability

The "Pay and Claim" Process

Scenario: You hired a lawyer (RCM applicable)

Legal fee: ‚Çπ50,000

GST @ 18%: ‚Çπ9,000

Step 1: Pay ‚Çπ9,000 from cash ledger to govt

Step 2: Declare ‚Çπ9,000 in GSTR-3B Table 3.1(d)

Step 3: Claim ‚Çπ9,000 ITC in GSTR-3B Table 4(A)(3)

Result: Net effect = Zero (paid ‚Çπ9,000, claimed ‚Çπ9,000 ITC)

When ITC is NOT Available

  • ‚ùå Goods/services used for personal purposes
  • ‚ùå Used for exempt supplies
  • ‚ùå Blocked credits under Section 17(5) (like food, health services)
  • ‚ùå You are composition dealer

Cash Flow Impact

Important: Even though you get ITC back, you still need upfront cash to pay RCM. Small businesses may face working capital issues.

Example: Pay ‚Çπ1 lakh RCM today from cash, get ‚Çπ1 lakh ITC in same month. No net tax, but need cash liquidity.

```

Compliance Requirements for RCM

```

Invoice Documentation

Supplier's Invoice Must Mention:

  • "Supply under Reverse Charge"
  • Invoice value WITHOUT adding GST
  • Supplier's details (name, address)
  • Supplier's GSTIN (if registered)

Recipient Must Maintain:

  • Supplier's invoice
  • Self-invoice (if supplier is unregistered)
  • Proof of GST payment under RCM
  • Books of accounts showing RCM transactions

GSTR-3B Filing

Both RCM liability and ITC must be shown in the same month's GSTR-3B:

  • Don't skip reporting RCM in Table 3.1(d)
  • Claim ITC immediately in Table 4(A)(3)
  • Pay the tax from cash ledger before filing

GSTR-2A vs GSTR-2B

RCM transactions do NOT appear in GSTR-2A/2B because supplier doesn't file details. You must self-report.

Annual Return Reporting

RCM details must be reported in GSTR-9 (annual return) in specific tables for RCM supplies.

üí° Best Practice: Maintain a separate register for all RCM transactions with columns: Date, Supplier, Invoice No., Amount, GST Rate, Tax Paid, ITC Claimed, Status.

```

Frequently Asked Questions

```

Can I use ITC to pay RCM?

No. RCM must be paid from electronic cash ledger only. You cannot use available ITC credit to pay RCM. This is a key restriction under Section 49(4) of CGST Act.

What if supplier charges GST on RCM supply?

If supplier wrongly charges GST on RCM supply, both parties may be liable. Supplier must refund the wrongly charged GST. Recipient should not pay GST to supplier and instead pay directly to government under RCM.

Is RCM applicable to composition dealers?

Composition dealers cannot avail ITC, so RCM becomes an additional cost for them. However, they still need to comply with RCM provisions if they receive notified goods/services.

Do I need to pay RCM on purchases from unregistered suppliers?

Currently NO, for most goods and services. Section 9(4) (RCM on unregistered supplier to registered recipient) has been suspended indefinitely. Only notified categories (like cashew nuts, tobacco from agriculturists) attract RCM.

How to report RCM in GSTR-1?

Generally, RCM transactions are not reported in GSTR-1 by the recipient. Only specific scenarios like e-commerce operators or import of services may require GSTR-1 reporting in certain tables.

What if I forget to pay RCM?

You'll be liable for interest @ 18% per annum from due date until payment. Plus late fee and potential penalty. Pay immediately and report in next GSTR-3B with interest.

Can I claim refund of RCM paid in error?

Yes, you can file refund claim if RCM was paid by mistake (e.g., supplier was registered, or service not under RCM). File refund within 2 years of payment date.

```
