Quick Summary: GST (Goods and Services Tax) is India's unified indirect tax system that replaced 17 different taxes. Implemented in 2017, it simplifies taxation with rates of 0%, 5%, 12%, 18%, and 28%.

What is GST?

```

GST (Goods and Services Tax) is a comprehensive indirect tax levied on the supply of goods and services in India. Introduced on July 1, 2017, GST replaced multiple cascading taxes charged by central and state governments.

Think of GST as a single tax that covers everything from manufacturing to final sale. Instead of paying different taxes at each stage (excise duty during manufacturing, VAT during sale, service tax for services), businesses now pay one unified tax.

GST Full Form

Goods and Services Tax - A destination-based tax on consumption of goods and services.

```

Types of GST in India

```

GST is divided into three types based on the nature of the transaction:

1. CGST (Central GST)

Collected by the Central Government on intra-state sales (within the same state).

Example: Sale in Mumbai to Pune (both Maharashtra) attracts CGST + SGST

2. SGST (State GST)

Collected by the State Government on intra-state sales.

Example: Same transaction above - State gets SGST portion

3. IGST (Integrated GST)

Collected by the Central Government on inter-state sales (between different states).

Example: Sale from Delhi to Mumbai attracts IGST only

```

Common GST Terms Explained

```

GSTIN (GST Identification Number)

15-digit unique identification number assigned to each registered taxpayer

HSN Code (Harmonized System of Nomenclature)

International product classification code used on GST invoices

Input Tax Credit (ITC)

Credit claimed for GST paid on business purchases, reducing final tax liability

Reverse Charge Mechanism (RCM)

Buyer pays GST instead of supplier in specific scenarios
```
