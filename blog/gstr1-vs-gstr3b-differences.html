GSTR-1 vs GSTR-3B: Key Differences Explained (2026)

Quick Summary: GSTR-1 is for reporting outward supplies (sales) filed by 11th. GSTR-3B is a summary return with tax payment filed by 20th. Both are monthly/quarterly. GSTR-1 data auto-populates in recipient's GSTR-2B. Mismatch between them causes ITC issues.

What is GSTR-1?

```

Definition

GSTR-1 is a monthly or quarterly return containing details of all outward supplies (sales) made by a registered taxpayer. It includes invoice-level information of all B2B, B2C, and export transactions.

Who Must File?

  • Monthly filers: Turnover > ‚Çπ5 crores
  • Quarterly filers: Turnover ‚â§ ‚Çπ5 crores (QRMP scheme)
  • Exempt: Composition dealers, Input Service Distributors

Sales >‚Çπ2.5 lakhs to consumers (invoice-wise)

3

B2C Small Invoices

Consolidated sales <‚Çπ2.5 lakhs (state-wise summary)

4

With and without payment of IGST

5

For amendments to previously issued invoices

6

Supplies with 0% GST or exempt items
```

What is GSTR-3B?

```

Definition

GSTR-3B is a simplified summary return where taxpayers declare their total outward supplies, ITC claimed, and pay GST. Unlike GSTR-1, it doesn't require invoice-level details‚Äîjust consolidated figures.

Who Must File?

  • Monthly filers: Turnover > ‚Çπ5 crores
  • Quarterly filers: Turnover ‚â§ ‚Çπ5 crores (with monthly tax payment)
  • Exempt: Composition dealers file GSTR-4 instead

What's Included in GSTR-3B?

1

Outward Supplies

Total taxable value + GST (summarized)

2

ITC Claimed

Input Tax Credit on purchases (as per GSTR-2B)

3

ITC Reversed

Credit reversed due to ineligible conditions

4

Net GST Liability

Tax payable after ITC adjustment

5

Interest & Late Fee

If applicable for delayed filing/payment

6

Payment

Tax, interest, penalty paid via cash or credit ledger
```

10 Key Differences Between GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B

```

1. Nature of Return

GSTR-1

Detailed Return

Contains invoice-by-invoice breakdown of all sales transactions.

GSTR-3B

Summary Return

Only consolidated totals, no invoice-level details required.

2. Purpose

GSTR-1

Information Sharing

To inform buyers about their purchases so they can claim ITC.

GSTR-3B

Tax Payment

To declare liability and pay GST to the government.

3. Payment Required?

GSTR-1

No Payment

Just declaration of sales. No tax payment involved.

GSTR-3B

Payment Mandatory

Must pay tax liability before filing. Cannot file without payment.

4. Filing Sequence

GSTR-1

File First

Due 11th (monthly) or 13th (quarterly). Should ideally be filed before GSTR-3B.

GSTR-3B

File Second

Due 20th (monthly) or 22nd/24th (quarterly). Filed after GSTR-1.

5. Impact on Buyers

GSTR-1

Direct Impact

Your GSTR-1 invoices appear in buyer's GSTR-2B. If wrong, they can't claim ITC.

GSTR-3B

No Direct Impact

Doesn't affect buyers. It's your own tax declaration to the government.

6. Data Source

GSTR-1

Your Sales Register

Manual entry from your sales invoices and accounts.

GSTR-3B

Multiple Sources

Outward supplies from books, ITC from GSTR-2B, payments from ledgers.

7. Revision Allowed?

GSTR-1

Partial Revision

Can amend invoices in next GSTR-1 via credit/debit notes (Table 9).

GSTR-3B

No Revision

Cannot be revised once filed. Correct errors in next month's return.

8. Penalty for Non-Filing

GSTR-1

‚Çπ100/day

(‚Çπ50 CGST + ‚Çπ50 SGST)

Capped at ‚Çπ5,000

GSTR-3B

‚Çπ100/day + 18% interest

(‚Çπ50 CGST + ‚Çπ50 SGST late fee)

Capped at ‚Çπ5,000 + interest on unpaid tax (no cap)

9. Can File NIL Return?

GSTR-1

Yes (SMS/Portal)

If no outward supplies, file NIL return via SMS or portal.

GSTR-3B

Yes (Portal Only)

Must file on portal even if NIL. SMS not allowed.

10. Blocking Impact

GSTR-1

ITC Blocked for Buyer

If you don't file, buyers can't claim ITC on your invoices.

GSTR-3B

Registration at Risk

Continuous non-filing may lead to GST registration cancellation.

```

How to Reconcile GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B

```

Why Reconciliation Matters

Ideally, outward supplies in GSTR-3B (Table 3.1) = total sales in GSTR-1. Mismatches trigger notices from GST department and can block ITC for your buyers.

Common Mismatch Reasons

  • Invoices included in GSTR-3B but missed in GSTR-1
  • Credit/debit notes not adjusted properly
  • B2C sales reported differently in both returns
  • Export values not matching
  • Amendments not carried forward correctly
```

Common Mistakes to Avoid

```

‚ùå Mistake #1: Filing GSTR-3B Without GSTR-1

Problem: Buyers can't see invoices in GSTR-2B, ITC blocked

Solution: Always file GSTR-1 before or alongside GSTR-3B

‚ùå Mistake #2: Not Matching Totals

Problem: GST notices for mismatch, possible tax demand

Solution: Reconcile both returns before final submission

‚ùå Mistake #3: Forgetting Credit/Debit Notes

Problem: Wrong taxable value, excess tax payment

Solution: Report all amendments in GSTR-1 Table 9 and adjust GSTR-3B

‚ùå Mistake #4: Claiming ITC Before Supplier Files GSTR-1

Problem: ITC reversal risk, interest liability

Solution: Only claim ITC visible in GSTR-2B (after supplier files GSTR-1)

‚ùå Mistake #5: Not Reversing Ineligible ITC

Problem: Wrong ITC claim, penalty, interest

Solution: Use GSTR-3B Table 4(B) to reverse ineligible ITC

‚ùå Mistake #6: Filing NIL GSTR-3B When Sales Exist

Problem: Tax evasion charge, heavy penalty

Solution: Declare all supplies even if small, never file fake NIL returns
```

Frequently Asked Questions

```

Can I file GSTR-3B before GSTR-1?

Yes, technically you can. There's no system restriction. However, it's NOT recommended because your buyers won't see invoices in their GSTR-2B until you file GSTR-1, which blocks their ITC claims.

What if GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B values don't match?

Mismatches trigger GST department notices. You'll need to explain the difference and correct errors in subsequent returns. Keep reconciliation documents ready. Persistent mismatches may lead to scrutiny and audits.

Do I need to file both returns every month?

If turnover > ‚Çπ5 crores: Yes, both monthly. If turnover ‚â§ ‚Çπ5 crores and enrolled in QRMP: GSTR-1 quarterly, GSTR-3B quarterly, but monthly tax payment via PMT-06.

Can I skip GSTR-1 if I only have B2C sales?

No. GSTR-1 is mandatory for all registered taxpayers (except composition dealers). You must report B2C sales in Table 7 even if no B2B transactions.

Is GSTR-2 still required?

No. GSTR-2 (purchase return) has been suspended since 2017. Instead, use auto-populated GSTR-2B to claim ITC. GSTR-2B is a read-only statement‚Äîyou can't edit it.

What happens if I file GSTR-1 late but GSTR-3B on time?

You'll pay late fee for GSTR-1 (‚Çπ100/day, max ‚Çπ5,000). Your buyers may complain because their ITC is delayed. Your GSTR-3B is safe if filed on time.
```
