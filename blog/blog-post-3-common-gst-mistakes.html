Wrong HSN Code on Invoices

What happens: Business owners put random or incorrect HSN (Harmonised System of Nomenclature) codes on their invoices. Some skip it altogether thinking it's not important.

Why it's a problem: HSN codes determine your tax rate. A wrong code means you're charging the wrong GST rate ‚Äî which can lead to tax shortfall notices and audits.

What happens: After GST registration, many business owners forget to upload their bank account details on the GST portal within 30 days.

Why it's a problem: Rule 10A states that if bank details are not updated, your GST registration can be automatically suspended. This means you can't charge GST, can't claim input credits, and your buyers lose trust.

4

What happens: Business owners claim Input Tax Credit (ITC) on purchases that are actually for personal use ‚Äî like a car purchase, personal phone, or home renovation.

Why it's a problem: ITC is only allowed on purchases made strictly for business purposes. Claiming personal expenses as business = tax fraud in the eyes of the department.

5

What happens: Small sellers on Amazon, Flipkart, or Meesho think the platform handles all their GST. They don't register or file returns themselves.

Why it's a problem: E-commerce sellers must have their own GST registration regardless of turnover. The platform collects tax, but YOU are responsible for filing returns and staying compliant.
