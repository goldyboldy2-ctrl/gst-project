GST Registration Process in India 2026

Quick Summary: GST registration is mandatory if your turnover exceeds ‚Çπ40 lakhs (‚Çπ20 lakhs for services). The online process takes 2-6 working days. Complete guide with documents, steps, and tips below.

What is GST Registration?

GST registration is the process of obtaining a unique 15-digit GSTIN (GST Identification Number) from the government. Once registered, you can legally collect GST from customers, claim input tax credit, and file GST returns.

Registration is done online through the GST portal and typically takes 2-6 working days for approval.

Who Must Register for GST?

1. Based on Turnover

Regular States: Annual turnover exceeds ‚Çπ40 lakhs (goods) or ‚Çπ20 lakhs (services)

Special Category States: Annual turnover exceeds ‚Çπ20 lakhs (goods) or ‚Çπ10 lakhs (services)

Special states: Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Sikkim, Tripura, Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, Jammu & Kashmir

2. Inter-State Supply

Mandatory registration even with ‚Çπ1 turnover if you make inter-state supplies (selling to other states).

3. E-commerce Sellers

All sellers on Amazon, Flipkart, etc. must register for GST regardless of turnover.

4. Reverse Charge Mechanism

Businesses liable to pay tax under reverse charge must register.

5. Other Mandatory Cases

  • Casual taxable person
  • Non-resident taxable person
  • Input Service Distributor
  • E-commerce aggregators
  • Agent/TDS deductor

GST Registration Process (Step-by-Step)

Follow these steps to complete your GST registration online:

1

Visit GST Portal

Go to www.gst.gov.in and click on "Services" ‚Üí "Registration" ‚Üí "New Registration"

2

Fill Part A - Basic Details

Enter basic information:

  • Legal name (as per PAN)
  • PAN number
  • Email ID
  • Mobile number
  • State
3

OTP Verification

Verify mobile number and email via OTP. You'll receive a Temporary Reference Number (TRN).

4

Fill Part B - Detailed Application

Login using TRN and complete detailed form:

  • Business details (name, type, constitution)
  • Principal place of business address
  • Additional places of business (if any)
  • Promoter/Partner/Director details with Aadhaar
  • Bank account details
  • Authorized signatory details
  • Goods/Services details (HSN/SAC codes)
5

Upload Documents

Upload scanned copies (PDF/JPEG, max 1MB each):

  • Photograph of proprietor/partners
  • Business address proof
  • Bank statement/cancelled cheque
  • Authorization letter (if applicable)
  • Constitution document
6

Verification

Add verification declaration stating all information is correct. E-sign using Aadhaar OTP or Digital Signature.

7

Submit Application

Submit the application. You'll receive an Application Reference Number (ARN) via SMS and email.

8

Get GSTIN

GST officer verifies application within 2-6 working days. If approved, GSTIN certificate is issued and sent via email. If rejected, clarification notice (GST REG-03) is sent - respond within 7 days.

Types of GST Registration

Regular Registration

For businesses with turnover above threshold. Can charge GST, claim full ITC, file monthly/quarterly returns.

Composition Scheme

For small businesses with turnover up to ‚Çπ1.5 crore. Pay flat 1-6% tax, no ITC, quarterly filing. Simpler compliance.

Casual Taxable Person

For temporary business in a state (like exhibitions, fairs). Valid for max 90 days.

Non-Resident Taxable Person

For foreign businesses making taxable supplies in India. Valid for max 90 days (extendable).

After Registration - Next Steps

Once you receive GSTIN:

  • Download GST Certificate: Keep digital and physical copies
  • Display GSTIN: On shop/office, website, invoices
  • Create GST-Compliant Invoices: Include GSTIN, HSN codes, tax breakdown
  • Start Filing Returns: GSTR-1 (11th), GSTR-3B (20th) every month
  • Maintain Records: All invoices, purchase bills for 6 years

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is GST registration free?

A: Yes, GST registration is completely free. No fees charged by government. If using CA, only their service charges apply.

Q: Can I register voluntarily below threshold?

A: Yes, voluntary registration is allowed. Benefits include claiming ITC, expanding to e-commerce, building business credibility.

Q: How many GSTINs can one person have?

A: One GSTIN per state. If you operate in 3 states, you need 3 separate registrations (one per state).

Q: What if application is rejected?

A: You'll receive GST REG-03 clarification notice. Respond with correct documents within 7 working days. If still rejected, you can file GST REG-05 appeal.
