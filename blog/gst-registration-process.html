Quick Summary

GST registration is mandatory if your turnover exceeds ‚Çπ40 lakhs (‚Çπ20 lakhs for services). The online process takes 2-6 working days. Complete guide with documents, steps, and tips below.

What is GST Registration?

GST registration is the process of obtaining a unique 15-digit GSTIN (GST Identification Number) from the government. Once registered, you can legally collect GST from customers, claim input tax credit, and file GST returns.

Registration is done online through the GST portal and typically takes 2-6 working days for approval.

Who Must Register for GST?

1. Based on Turnover

Regular States: Annual turnover exceeds ‚Çπ40 lakhs (goods) or ‚Çπ20 lakhs (services)

Special Category States: Annual turnover exceeds ‚Çπ20 lakhs (goods) or ‚Çπ10 lakhs (services)

Special states: Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Sikkim, Tripura, Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, J&K

2. Inter-State Supply

Mandatory registration even with ‚Çπ1 turnover if you make inter-state supplies.

3. E-commerce Sellers

All sellers on Amazon, Flipkart, etc. must register regardless of turnover.

4. Other Mandatory Cases

  • Reverse charge mechanism liable persons
  • Casual taxable person
  • Non-resident taxable person
  • E-commerce aggregators
  • Agent/TDS deductor

Documents Required

  • PAN Card: Of business owner/proprietor
  • Aadhaar Card: For authentication
  • Business Proof: Certificate of incorporation, partnership deed, or proprietorship declaration
  • Address Proof: Rent agreement, electricity bill, property tax receipt
  • Bank Details: Cancelled cheque or bank statement
  • Photos: Passport-size photos of owners/partners
  • Digital Signature: For companies/LLPs

Step-by-Step Registration Process

Step 1: Visit GST Portal

Go to www.gst.gov.in and click "Register Now"

Step 2: Part A - OTP Verification

Enter PAN, mobile, email. Verify OTP. System generates TRN (Temporary Reference Number)

Step 3: Part B - Complete Application

Login with TRN. Fill business details, owner/partner details, address, bank account, upload documents

Step 4: Verification & DSC

Sign application using DSC (companies/LLPs) or EVC (proprietors/partnerships)

Step 5: ARN Generation

Application submitted. ARN (Application Reference Number) generated. Track status on portal

Step 6: Approval & GSTIN

Verification by tax officer (2-6 working days). GSTIN certificate issued via email
