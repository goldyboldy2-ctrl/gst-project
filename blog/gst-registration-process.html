Need to register for GST? This comprehensive guide walks you through the entire GST registration process in India - from checking eligibility to receiving your GSTIN. Whether you're a startup, sole proprietor, or existing business crossing the turnover threshold, this guide has you covered.

Who Must Register for GST?

GST registration is mandatory if you meet any of these criteria:

  • Turnover exceeds â‚¹40 lakhs (â‚¹20 lakhs for special category states) for goods suppliers
  • Turnover exceeds â‚¹20 lakhs (â‚¹10 lakhs for special category states) for service providers
  • Engaged in interstate supply of goods or services (regardless of turnover)
  • Operating an e-commerce business (even with zero turnover)
  • Making taxable supplies on behalf of another taxable person (agents)
  • Casual taxable person conducting business temporarily
  • Input Service Distributor
  • Supplying goods/services via e-commerce aggregators
Pro Tip: Even if registration isn't mandatory, you can opt for voluntary registration to claim Input Tax Credit (ITC) and appear more credible to customers. Many B2B buyers prefer GST-registered vendors.

Documents Required for GST Registration

Gather these documents before starting your application:

For Proprietorship:

  • PAN Card of the proprietor
  • Aadhaar Card of the proprietor
  • Photograph of the proprietor
  • Address proof of business premises (rent agreement, electricity bill, property tax receipt)
  • Bank account statement or cancelled cheque
  • Digital signature (if applicable)

For Partnership Firm / LLP:

  • PAN Card of the firm
  • Partnership deed or LLP agreement
  • PAN and Aadhaar of all partners
  • Photograph of all partners
  • Address proof of business premises
  • Bank account details
  • Authorization letter for authorized signatory

For Private Limited / Public Limited Company:

  • PAN Card of the company
  • Certificate of Incorporation
  • Memorandum of Association (MOA)
  • Articles of Association (AOA)
  • PAN and Aadhaar of directors
  • Photograph of directors
  • Address proof of registered office
  • Bank account details
  • Board resolution for authorized signatory

Step-by-Step GST Registration Process

Step 1: Visit the GST Portal

Go to https://www.gst.gov.in and click on "Services" â†’ "Registration" â†’ "New Registration".

Step 2: Fill Part A (OTP Verification)

Enter the following details:

  • Select taxpayer category (Regular, Composition, etc.)
  • Enter PAN of business/proprietor
  • Enter mobile number and email address
  • Verify with OTP sent to mobile and email

Result: You'll receive a Temporary Reference Number (TRN) via email and SMS. This is valid for 15 days.

Step 3: Fill Part B (Detailed Application)

Login using TRN and complete the detailed application form with:

  • Business details: Legal name, trade name, constitution, date of commencement
  • Promoter/Partner details: Names, PAN, Aadhaar, photographs
  • Principal place of business: Complete address with proof
  • Additional places of business: If applicable (godowns, branches)
  • Authorized signatory: Details of person signing on behalf of business
  • Bank account details: Account number, IFSC, bank name
  • Goods/Services supplied: HSN/SAC codes of main products/services

Step 4: Upload Required Documents

Upload scanned copies in PDF/JPEG format (max 1MB each):

  • Photograph of proprietor/partners/directors
  • Proof of business address (rent agreement, electricity bill, property documents)
  • Bank account statement or cancelled cheque
  • Authorization letter for authorized signatory (if different from proprietor)
  • Constitution-specific documents (partnership deed, incorporation certificate, etc.)

Step 5: Verification and E-Sign

Two options to verify your application:

  • Option 1: E-sign using Aadhaar OTP (simpler and faster)
  • Option 2: Use Digital Signature Certificate (DSC)

After verification, click "Submit" to file the application.

Step 6: ARN Generation

Upon successful submission, you'll receive an Application Reference Number (ARN) via email and SMS. Use this to track your application status.

Step 7: Processing by GST Officer

The GST officer will review your application. Three possible outcomes:

  • Approved: GSTIN issued within 3-7 working days
  • Clarification required: Officer may ask for additional documents (respond within 7 days)
  • Physical verification: In some cases, officer may visit your business premises

Step 8: Receive GSTIN Certificate

Once approved, you'll receive your 15-digit GSTIN (GST Identification Number) via email. Download the GST registration certificate from the portal.

Format: 27AAAAA0000A1Z5 (State Code + PAN + Entity Code + Check Digit)

GST Registration Timeline

The entire process typically takes:

  • 3-7 working days for straightforward applications with complete documents
  • 15-30 days if clarifications or additional documents are requested
  • 30+ days if physical verification is required or responses are delayed

Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Incorrect PAN Details

Ensure PAN entered matches exactly with PAN card. Even a minor typo will cause rejection.

2. Poor Quality Document Scans

Upload clear, legible scans. Blurry or cut-off documents lead to rejection. Use good lighting and high resolution.

3. Mismatched Addresses

Business address on documents should match the address entered in application. Use the same format.

4. Missing Mobile/Email OTP

Check spam folder if you don't receive OTP. Ensure mobile number and email are active and accessible.

5. Incomplete HSN/SAC Codes

Select appropriate codes for your business. Use our HSN Finder tool to find the right codes.

6. Not Responding to Clarifications

If officer requests additional info, respond within 7 days. Delayed response can lead to application rejection.

Post-Registration Compliance

After receiving your GSTIN, remember to:

  • Display GSTIN prominently at your business premises and on invoices
  • File monthly/quarterly returns as per your registration type (check our Return Deadlines tool)
  • Maintain proper records of all sales, purchases, and tax payments
  • Issue GST-compliant invoices for all taxable supplies (use our Invoice Generator)
  • Update registration if business details change (address, partners, bank account)

GST Registration Fees

Good news: GST registration is completely FREE. There are no government fees for:

  • New registration
  • Amendments to registration
  • Cancellation of registration

Note: If hiring a CA or consultant, they may charge professional fees (typically â‚¹2,000-â‚¹5,000), but the government process itself is free.

When Can You Cancel GST Registration?

You can apply for cancellation if:

  • Business is discontinued or transferred
  • Turnover falls below threshold limit
  • Composition scheme dealer discontinues business
  • Taxable person is no longer liable to be registered

File cancellation application on GST portal within 30 days of the event. You can continue using GSTIN until the cancellation is approved.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is Aadhaar mandatory for GST registration?

A: Yes, Aadhaar authentication is mandatory for proprietors, partners, directors, and authorized signatories. You can verify using Aadhaar OTP during the application process.

Q: Can I register before starting business?

A: Yes, you can register up to 30 days before commencing business operations. This is useful for planning and getting GSTIN for vendor onboarding.

Q: What if I have multiple business locations?

A: You need to obtain separate GSTIN for each state where you have a business presence. Within a state, you can have multiple business locations under one GSTIN.

Q: Can I register online without a CA?

A: Absolutely! The GST portal is user-friendly and designed for self-registration. Follow this guide step-by-step and you can complete it yourself. Hire a CA only if you have complex business structure or need professional assistance.

Q: What happens if my application is rejected?

A: You can reapply immediately with corrected information and proper documents. Learn from the rejection notice and fix the issues mentioned.
