What is GST Composition Scheme?

```

The Composition Scheme is a simplified GST compliance option for small businesses. Instead of paying GST at standard rates (5-28%), you pay a flat percentage of your turnover and enjoy reduced compliance burden.

Key Features

  • ‚úÖ Low tax rates: 1% to 6% of turnover (vs. up to 28% in regular scheme)
  • ‚úÖ Quarterly filing: Only GSTR-4 once every 3 months
  • ‚úÖ Simple accounting: No complex invoice matching
  • ‚úÖ Less paperwork: Minimal documentation requirements

Who Should Consider?

‚úÖ Good For

  • Small manufacturers
  • Local retailers/traders
  • Restaurants
  • B2C businesses
  • Low-margin businesses

‚ùå Not Suitable For

  • Service providers
  • E-commerce sellers
  • B2B businesses
  • Inter-state suppliers
  • Exporters
```

Eligible Businesses

  • ‚úÖ Manufacturers of goods (except ice cream, pan masala, tobacco)
  • ‚úÖ Traders (buying and selling goods)
  • ‚úÖ Restaurant services (not serving alcohol)
  • ‚úÖ Mixed suppliers (goods + restaurant service)

Who CANNOT Opt for Composition Scheme?

‚ö†Ô∏è Not Eligible:

  • ‚ùå Service providers (except restaurants)
  • ‚ùå Inter-state suppliers
  • ‚ùå Casual taxable persons
  • ‚ùå Non-resident taxable persons
  • ‚ùå E-commerce operators (through marketplace)
  • ‚ùå Suppliers of notified goods: Ice cream, pan masala, tobacco products
  • ‚ùå Manufacturers of goods specified in Section 2(d) of Central Excise Act

Additional Conditions

  1. Intra-state only: Cannot make inter-state sales
  2. No ITC: Cannot claim Input Tax Credit on purchases
  3. All GSTINs: If multiple registrations, all must be in composition
  4. B2C focus: Primarily supply to end consumers (unregistered persons)

Limitations & Restrictions

```

Major Limitations

‚ùå No Input Tax Credit (ITC)

Impact: Cannot claim ITC on purchases, making it expensive if you buy taxable goods/services

Example: Buy raw material for ‚Çπ100 + ‚Çπ18 GST. Cannot claim ‚Çπ18 ITC. It becomes your cost.

‚ùå No Inter-State Sales

Impact: Can only sell within your state, limiting market reach

Example: Gujarat dealer cannot sell to Maharashtra customer

‚ùå B2B Customer Disadvantage

Impact: B2B customers cannot claim ITC on purchases from you

Example: Your customer buys ‚Çπ100 item from you. They cannot claim any ITC, making your product expensive for them

‚ùå Cannot Issue Tax Invoice

Impact: Must issue "Bill of Supply" only, limiting B2B business

Example: B2B buyers prefer tax invoices to claim ITC

‚ùå E-commerce Restriction

Impact: Cannot sell through Amazon, Flipkart, etc. (marketplace model)

Note: Own website sales within state are allowed

‚ùå Service Provider Restriction

Impact: Most service businesses cannot opt (except restaurants)

Example: Consultants, software developers, agencies cannot use composition

When NOT to Opt for Composition Scheme

  • üö´ If you make inter-state sales
  • üö´ If major customers are B2B (they need tax invoices)
  • üö´ If you're a service provider (except restaurant)
  • üö´ If your profit margin is very high (ITC loss won't matter much)
  • üö´ If you plan to grow beyond ‚Çπ1.5 crore turnover soon
  • üö´ If you buy heavily taxed inputs (ITC loss will be high)
```

  1. File intimation in CMP-04 within 7 days
  2. Switch to regular scheme from date of exceeding limit

Frequently Asked Questions

```

Can I switch between regular and composition scheme anytime?

No. You can opt-in or opt-out only once per financial year by filing CMP-02 or CMP-04 before 31st March. Exception: If turnover exceeds ‚Çπ1.5 crores, you must switch to regular immediately.

What happens to ITC on stock when I opt for composition?

You must reverse ITC claimed on closing stock, semi-finished goods, and capital goods. File ITC-03 within 60 days. The reversed amount must be paid back to the government.

Can I sell on Amazon/Flipkart under composition scheme?

No for marketplace model (where Amazon/Flipkart is seller). Yes for your own seller account if sales are only intra-state. Most e-commerce is inter-state, so composition is not practical.

Is composition scheme good for my business?

It depends. Good if: (1) Mostly B2C sales, (2) Low input GST, (3) Intra-state only, (4) Want simple compliance. Not good if: Major B2B customers, Inter-state sales, High input costs.

Can I show GST separately in Bill of Supply?

No. Bill of Supply shows only total value without GST breakup. You cannot collect tax from customer separately. Tax is included in your sale price and paid from your profit.

Do I still need to pay GST if no profit?

Yes. Composition tax is on turnover, not profit. Even if you make loss, 1-6% GST applies on total sales value.

```
