GST compliance can be challenging for businesses. Small mistakes can lead to penalties, interest, and compliance notices. Here are the 10 most common GST mistakes and how to avoid them.

Mistake #1: Missing GST Registration Deadline

The Problem: Many businesses continue operating after crossing the ‚Çπ40 lakh (goods) or ‚Çπ20 lakh (services) turnover threshold without registering for GST.

Mistake #2: Incorrect GSTIN on Invoices

The Problem: Typing errors in supplier or customer GSTIN on invoices prevents ITC claims and causes mismatches in GSTR-2A/2B.

Impact: Buyer cannot claim ITC, supplier faces notices

‚úÖ Solution

Verify GSTIN using our GSTIN Verification Tool before issuing invoices. Double-check all 15 characters. Implement automated validation in your billing software.

Mistake #3: Claiming ITC on Blocked Items

The Problem: Businesses claim ITC on items blocked under Section 17(5) such as motor vehicles for personal use, food & beverages, club memberships.

Penalty: ITC reversal + 18% interest + potential penalty

‚úÖ Solution

Know Section 17(5) restrictions. Use our ITC Calculator to identify blocked items. Maintain separate records for eligible and blocked ITC.

Mistake #4: Late Filing of Returns

The Problem: Missing GSTR-1 (11th) or GSTR-3B (20th) deadlines even by one day.

Penalty: ‚Çπ50/day (‚Çπ20/day if turnover < ‚Çπ1.5 Cr), max ‚Çπ5,000 per return

Mistake #5: Mismatch Between GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B

The Problem: Sales figures in GSTR-1 don't match output tax liability declared in GSTR-3B.

Impact: System-generated notices, potential audit

‚úÖ Solution

Reconcile before filing GSTR-3B. Check GSTR-1 data is reflected correctly. Use our GSTR-3B Helper for accurate calculations.

Impact: Buyer claims wrong ITC, supplier faces notices

Mistake #8: Ignoring Reverse Charge Mechanism (RCM)

The Problem: Not paying GST under RCM when purchasing from unregistered suppliers or on notified services (GTA, legal services).

Identify RCM transactions. Pay tax in GSTR-3B Table 3.1(d). Know RCM categories: GTA services, legal services, security services, purchases from unregistered persons >‚Çπ5,000/day.
