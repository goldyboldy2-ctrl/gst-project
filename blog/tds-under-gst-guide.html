Tax Deduction at Source (TDS) under GST is a mechanism where certain notified persons deduct a percentage of GST from payments made to suppliers and deposit it with the government on behalf of the supplier.

Frequently Asked Questions

```

Is TDS under GST same as TDS under Income Tax?

No. They are completely different. TDS under GST is on supply value and credited to GST ledger. TDS under Income Tax is on salary/professional fees and credited to Income Tax. Both can apply simultaneously on same transaction.

Can I claim refund of TDS if I have no GST liability?

Yes. If TDS credit exceeds your GST liability for a particular period, you can file refund claim for the excess credit. Refund is processed like normal GST refund.

What if deductor does not deposit TDS to government?

Deductor is liable for interest and penalty. Supplier should follow up with deductor and check GSTR-7A. If TDS not deposited, credit won't reflect in supplier's ledger.

Is TDS applicable on advance payments?

No. TDS is deducted only on final payment after supply is made. Not applicable on advances.

Can composition dealers claim TDS credit?

Yes. Even composition dealers can claim TDS credit in electronic credit ledger and use it to pay their quarterly composition tax.

What if wrong TDS amount is deducted?

Deductor can correct in next month's GSTR-7. If excess TDS deducted, can be adjusted. If less TDS deducted, balance must be deposited with interest.

```
