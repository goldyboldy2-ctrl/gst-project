GST in India has five tax slabs: 0%, 5%, 12%, 18%, and 28%. Understanding which products and services fall under which slab is crucial for accurate compliance and invoicing.

GST Tax Slabs Overview

Tax Slab Category Examples
0% (Nil) Exempted Fresh vegetables, milk, bread, education, healthcare
5% Essential items Edible oil, tea, coffee, spices, packaged food
12% Standard goods Computers, processed food, mobile phones
18% Most goods/services Electronics, IT services, restaurants, soap
28% Luxury/sin goods Cars, AC, cigarettes, aerated drinks + cess

üí° Key Principle

Essential items ‚Üí Lower tax. Luxury items ‚Üí Higher tax. This ensures GST is progressive and affordable for common people.

Food Items:

Inverted Duty Structure

When input tax rate > output tax rate, it creates inverted duty:

Example

Purchase raw material @ 18% GST

Sell finished product @ 12% GST

Result: ITC accumulates (can claim refund)

Frequently Asked Questions

Can GST rates change during the year?

Yes. GST Council meets periodically and can change rates. Changes are notified and effective from specified dates.

What if my product fits multiple categories?

Use the most specific HSN code. If still unclear, consult the GST portal's classification or a tax expert.

Are GST rates uniform across India?

Yes. GST rates are the same in all states (CGST + SGST = IGST).

Where can I check the latest GST rates?

Official GST portal (gst.gov.in) has the latest rate schedule. Our HSN Finder tool also has updated rates.
