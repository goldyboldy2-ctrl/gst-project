What is E-way Bill?

An E-way Bill (Electronic Way Bill) is a digital document required for the movement of goods worth more than ‚Çπ50,000 from one place to another under GST. It can be generated on the E-way Bill Portal by the registered person or transporter.

```

Key Features

  • Electronic Document: Generated and tracked online
  • Unique Number: 12-digit E-way Bill Number (EBN)
  • Real-time Tracking: Movement can be tracked by authorities
  • Validity Based on Distance: Time-bound based on transportation distance

Legal Basis

E-way Bill is governed by:

  • Rule 138 of CGST Rules, 2017
  • Notification No. 13/2020: Distance and validity norms
  • State-specific rules: Some states have additional requirements

üí° Purpose: E-way Bills help prevent tax evasion by tracking goods movement and ensuring proper GST payment on inter-state and intra-state supplies.

```

```

Threshold Limit

E-way Bill is mandatory when:

  • Value of goods exceeds ‚Çπ50,000
  • Goods are moved from one place to another
  • Movement is for supply, return, or any other reason
```

Exemptions from E-way Bill

```

Goods Exempted

E-way Bill is NOT required for:

  1. Exempt goods under Schedule III (e.g., liquor for human consumption, petroleum products)
  2. Goods transported by non-motorized vehicle (bullock cart, bicycle)
  3. Goods moved by railways (from consignor to railway, railway to consignee exempted)
  4. Transit cargo to Nepal or Bhutan
  5. Household goods for personal use
  6. Goods value ‚â§ ‚Çπ50,000 (per consignment)

Specific Exemptions
```

E-way Bill Validity Calculation

```

Validity Rules (2026)

For Road Transport:

1 day for every 100 km or part thereof

For Other Modes (Rail/Air/Ship):

1 day for every 200 km or part thereof

Minimum Validity: 1 day

Calculation Examples

Example 1: Road Transport

  • Distance: 450 km
  • Formula: 450 √∑ 100 = 4.5 ‚Üí Round up to 5 days

Example 2: Rail Transport

  • Distance: 850 km
  • Formula: 850 √∑ 200 = 4.25 ‚Üí Round up to 5 days

Example 3: Short Distance

  • Distance: 45 km (road)
  • Formula: 45 √∑ 100 = 0.45 ‚Üí Minimum 1 day

Validity Extension

E-way Bill validity can be extended if:

  • Goods could not reach destination due to exceptional circumstances
  • Extension must be done before expiry (or within 8 hours after expiry)
  • Valid reasons: Natural calamity, accident, law and order issues, transshipment delay

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical: Movement of goods with expired E-way Bill is treated as movement without E-way Bill and attracts penalties.

Distance Calculation

Distance is calculated based on:

  • Shortest route: From origin PIN to destination PIN
  • System calculated: Portal auto-calculates based on PIN codes
  • Can be overridden: If actual route is longer, update manually
```

Part A and Part B of E-way Bill

```

Part A: Transaction Details

Filled by: Supplier/Consignor

Contains:

  • Supplier and recipient GSTIN
  • Invoice/challan details
  • Goods description and HSN code
  • Taxable value and GST amount
  • Reason for transportation

Part B: Vehicle Details

Filled by: Transporter or Vehicle owner

Contains:

  • Transporter ID/GSTIN
  • Transport document number
  • Vehicle number
  • Transport mode (Road/Rail/Air/Ship)
  • Approximate distance

Who Fills What?

Scenario Part A Part B
Own transport Supplier Supplier
Hired transporter Supplier Transporter
Recipient arranges transport Supplier Recipient/Transporter
Transporter to transporter First transporter Subsequent transporter

Vehicle Change During Transit

If vehicle breaks down or needs change:

  1. Update Part B with new vehicle number
  2. Can be done by transporter or supplier
  3. Original EBN remains same, only vehicle details update
  4. Update before moving goods in new vehicle

üí° Good Practice: Keep printed/digital copy of E-way Bill in the vehicle at all times during transit.
```

```
Detention and Seizure

If goods are moved without proper E-way Bill:

  • Goods detained: Vehicle stopped and goods held
  • Tax + Penalty payable: Before goods released
  • Vehicle detention: Until compliance
  • Prosecution: In case of intentional evasion

Release of Detained Goods

Process:

  1. Pay applicable tax + penalty
  2. Generate proper E-way Bill
  3. Provide documents to detaining officer
  4. Goods released within 7 days of compliance
```

Frequently Asked Questions

```

Can I generate E-way Bill without GST registration?

No, you need GSTIN to generate E-way Bill. However, if the recipient is unregistered, use "URP" (Unregistered Person) option and provide recipient's details including address and PIN code.

What if vehicle breaks down during transit?

You can update Part B of the E-way Bill with new vehicle details before transferring goods. The original EBN remains valid, and validity period doesn't change.

Is E-way Bill required for multiple invoices in one vehicle?

Yes, separate E-way Bill is required for each consignment/invoice if value exceeds ‚Çπ50,000. Alternatively, you can generate a Consolidated E-way Bill for multiple E-way Bills in one vehicle.

Can E-way Bill be cancelled?

Yes, E-way Bill can be cancelled within 24 hours of generation, provided goods have not been transported. After 24 hours, cancellation is not allowed.

Who should carry E-way Bill during transport?

The person in charge of the vehicle must carry either a physical printout or digital copy (on mobile) of the E-way Bill during transit.

Is E-way Bill required for goods below ‚Çπ50,000 if transported with goods above ‚Çπ50,000?

Each consignment is treated separately. If a particular consignment is below ‚Çπ50,000, E-way Bill is not required for that consignment, even if the total value in the vehicle exceeds ‚Çπ50,000.

What happens if E-way Bill expires during transit?

You must extend the E-way Bill validity before expiry. Extension can be done up to 8 hours after expiry in exceptional cases. Movement with expired E-way Bill attracts penalty.
```
