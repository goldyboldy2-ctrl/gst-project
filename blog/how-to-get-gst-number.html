Do You Even Need GST Registration?

Before you start, check if you actually need it. You must register if any of the following apply:

  • Annual turnover is more than ‚Çπ40 lakhs (goods) or ‚Çπ20 lakhs (services)
  • You sell products across state borders (e.g., ship from Maharashtra to Karnataka)
  • You sell on e-commerce platforms like Amazon, Flipkart, or Meesho
  • You import services from outside India
  • You are a casual or non-resident taxable person

If none of the above apply, registration is voluntary ‚Äî but can still be beneficial for claiming input tax credits.

Documents You'll Need

Keep these ready before you start. Upload them as PDFs or clear photos:

Step-by-Step: How to Apply

1

Go to the GST Portal

Visit www.gst.gov.in ‚Äî this is the only official website. Avoid any third-party sites that charge fees for "helping" you register.

2

Click "New Registration"

Go to Registration ‚Üí New Registration. Select "Regular Taxpayer" if you are a normal business. Choose "Composition" if your turnover is under ‚Çπ1.5 crore and you want a simpler scheme.

3

Enter Basic Details

Fill in your name, PAN, email, and mobile number. You'll receive an OTP on your mobile and email to verify. Keep both ready.

4

Fill in Business Details

Enter your business name, address, nature of business, and the HSN codes for products/services you deal in. Not sure about HSN codes? Google "[your product] HSN code India" to find it.

5

Upload Documents

Upload all documents listed above. Make sure photos are clear and readable. Blurry uploads = rejection and delays.

6

Submit and Wait

Review everything, then submit. You'll get an Application Reference Number (ARN). The government usually processes it in 2-7 working days.

7

Receive Your GSTIN

Once approved, your GSTIN (GST Identification Number) will be sent to your email. It's a 15-character alphanumeric code. Save it carefully ‚Äî you'll use it on every invoice.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

‚ö†Ô∏è Watch Out For These

1. Wrong address proof: Bill must be from the last 3 months. Old documents get rejected.

2. Name mismatch: Name on PAN, Aadhaar, and application must match exactly. Even small spelling differences cause rejection.

3. Using fake portals: Only use gst.gov.in. Many fake sites charge ‚Çπ2,000-5,000 to do what you can do for free.

4. Forgetting HSN codes: You must enter correct HSN codes for your products. Wrong codes cause compliance issues later.

5. Not updating bank details: After registration, update your bank account on the portal immediately. Under Rule 10A, failure to do this can suspend your registration.

What Happens After Registration?

Once you have your GSTIN, you need to:

  • Print it and display it at your place of business
  • Add it to all your invoices
  • File GST returns monthly or quarterly (depending on your turnover)
  • Keep all purchase and sales records for at least 6 years
