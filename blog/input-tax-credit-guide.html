Quick Summary

Input Tax Credit (ITC) allows businesses to reduce GST paid on purchases from GST collected on sales. Master ITC rules, blocked credits under Section 17(5), and compliance requirements to maximize savings.

What is Input Tax Credit?

Input Tax Credit (ITC) is a mechanism that allows businesses to claim credit for GST paid on purchases (inputs) and offset it against GST collected on sales (output).

This eliminates the cascading effect of taxes - you don't pay tax on tax!

Who Can Claim ITC?

You can claim ITC if you meet ALL these conditions:

  • ‚úÖ You are registered under GST
  • ‚úÖ You possess a valid tax invoice or debit note
  • ‚úÖ You have received the goods/services
  • ‚úÖ You have filed GST returns
  • ‚úÖ Supplier has filed their GST return (invoice appears in GSTR-2B)
  • ‚úÖ Tax has been paid to the government by supplier
  • ‚úÖ You paid the supplier within 180 days (Rule 37)

Rule 37 (180-Day Rule): If you don't pay supplier within 180 days, ITC must be reversed with 18% interest. This is a common audit trigger!

Blocked ITC - Section 17(5)

ITC is NOT available on the following (even if you have valid invoices):

1. Motor Vehicles & Conveyances

Blocked: Vehicles for personal use, employee transport

Allowed: Passenger transport business, driving schools, vehicles for further supply/renting

2. Food & Beverages

Blocked: Food/drinks for personal consumption

Allowed: For resale, hotels/restaurants, statutory employee obligation

3. Outdoor Catering

Blocked: Event catering, parties

Allowed: For further supply (caterers), statutory obligation

4. Health & Fitness Services

Blocked: Gym memberships, club memberships, health services

Allowed: Very limited - usually always blocked

5. Life Insurance & Health Insurance

Blocked: Life insurance for employees

Allowed: Health insurance may qualify in specific cases

6. Works Contract Services (for immovable property)

blocked: Construction, renovation of buildings

Allowed: Plant & machinery installation

How to Claim ITC?

Step 1: Check GSTR-2B

GSTR-2B is your auto-generated purchase register. Only invoices in GSTR-2B can be claimed as ITC.

Step 2: Reconcile with Books

Match GSTR-2B with your purchase records. Identify missing invoices.

Step 3: Calculate Eligible ITC

Subtract blocked credits (Section 17(5)) and proportionate reversal for exempt supplies.

Step 4: File GSTR-3B

Claim eligible ITC in Table 4 of GSTR-3B.

Step 5: Maintain Documents

Keep invoices, payment proof, goods receipt notes for 6 years.

Common ITC Mistakes to Avoid

  • ‚ùå Claiming ITC without checking GSTR-2B
  • ‚ùå Claiming ITC on blocked categories
  • ‚ùå Not reversing ITC when payment not made in 180 days
  • ‚ùå Claiming ITC on exempt supplies proportion
  • ‚ùå Not maintaining proper documentation
  • ‚ùå Claiming ITC before receiving goods/services
  • ‚ùå Claiming ITC on invoices where supplier hasn't filed return

ITC Best Practices

  • ‚úÖ Monthly reconciliation: Compare GSTR-2B with books every month
  • ‚úÖ Track 180-day rule: Use payment tracker, pay within 150 days
  • ‚úÖ Document everything: Keep digital copies of invoices & receipts
  • ‚úÖ Use GSTR-2B: Only claim what appears in auto-populated return
  • ‚úÖ Reverse blocked credits: Don't claim ITC on Section 17(5) items
  • ‚úÖ Follow-up with suppliers: Ensure they file returns on time
