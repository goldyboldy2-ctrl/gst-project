Input Tax Credit (ITC) Explained: Complete Guide 2026

‚≠ê Advanced Topics ‚Ä¢ Feb 10, 2026 ‚Ä¢ 12 min read

Input Tax Credit (ITC) is one of the most powerful features of GST - it eliminates tax-on-tax and reduces your overall tax liability. But it's also one of the most misunderstood concepts. This guide explains everything about ITC: what it is, how to claim it, conditions, reversal rules, and common mistakes.

What is Input Tax Credit?

Input Tax Credit means the GST you paid on purchases can be used to offset the GST you need to pay on sales. Instead of paying tax on tax, you only pay tax on the value you added.

üìä Simple Example

Scenario: You manufacture furniture

  • Bought raw materials for ‚Çπ1,00,000 + ‚Çπ18,000 GST (18%)
  • Sold finished furniture for ‚Çπ2,00,000 + ‚Çπ36,000 GST (18%)

Without ITC: You pay ‚Çπ36,000 to government (ouch!)

With ITC: You pay ‚Çπ36,000 - ‚Çπ18,000 = ‚Çπ18,000 only (much better!)

Key Principle: You get credit for the tax you already paid, so you don't pay double tax on the same value.

Types of Input Tax Credit

1. Tax Credit on Inputs

Credit on goods purchased for business - raw materials, consumables, packing materials, spare parts, etc.

2. Tax Credit on Input Services

Credit on services used for business - rent, electricity, professional fees, freight, advertising, etc.

3. Tax Credit on Capital Goods

Credit on fixed assets - machinery, equipment, furniture, computers, vehicles (with conditions), etc.

Conditions to Claim Input Tax Credit

To claim ITC, all these conditions must be met (Section 16 of CGST Act):

‚úì Condition 1: You must possess a valid tax invoice or debit note
‚úì Condition 2: You have received the goods/services (physical possession)
‚úì Condition 3: Tax has been actually paid to the government by your supplier
‚úì Condition 4: You have filed your GST return (GSTR-3B)
‚úì Condition 5: Supplier has reported the invoice in their GSTR-1
‚ö†Ô∏è Critical: If your supplier hasn't paid GST to the government or hasn't filed GSTR-1, you cannot claim ITC - even if you paid them! This is why vendor verification is crucial. Use our GSTIN Verification tool.

Time Limit to Claim ITC

You can claim ITC on an invoice until the earlier of these two dates:

  • Due date of filing GSTR-3B return for the month of September following the financial year (usually October 20), OR
  • Date of filing annual return (GSTR-9) for that financial year

Example: Time Limit

Invoice dated: March 15, 2025 (FY 2024-25)

Last date to claim ITC: October 20, 2025 (due date of September 2025 GSTR-3B)

If you miss this deadline, ITC on that invoice is lost forever!

Blocked Credits - When You CANNOT Claim ITC

Section 17(5) specifically blocks ITC on certain items, even if you have valid invoices:

‚ùå Motor Vehicles

ITC is blocked on motor vehicles (cars, bikes, etc.) except:

  • Vehicles used for passenger transport services (taxis, ride-sharing)
  • Vehicles used for goods transportation (delivery vans, trucks)
  • Vehicles used for imparting training (driving schools)
  • Vehicles used for further supply of such vehicles (dealers)
Example: If you buy a ‚Çπ10,00,000 car + ‚Çπ1,80,000 GST for office use (employees commuting), you cannot claim the ‚Çπ1,80,000 ITC. But if you're a taxi service, you can claim it.

‚ùå Food and Beverages

ITC blocked on food, beverages, outdoor catering, beauty treatment, health services, cosmetic and plastic surgery except when used for making outward taxable supplies of the same category.

‚ùå Works Contract Services

ITC blocked on works contract for construction of immovable property except:

  • Plant and machinery
  • Construction of property for sale (builders)

‚ùå Membership of Clubs, Health and Fitness Centers

Gym memberships, club memberships - ITC fully blocked.

‚ùå Goods Lost, Stolen, Destroyed

If goods are lost, stolen, destroyed, or written off, ITC must be reversed.

‚ùå Personal Use

Anything used for personal consumption - no ITC.

ITC Reversal - When to Return ITC

In certain situations, you must reverse (return) ITC already claimed:

1. Non-Payment to Supplier

If you don't pay your supplier within 180 days from invoice date, reverse ITC. You can reclaim it when you pay.

2. Common Credits (Mixed Use)

If you use inputs/services for both taxable and exempt supplies, reverse proportionate ITC on exempt supplies.

Example: Reversal for Mixed Use

Total purchases: ‚Çπ10,00,000 + ‚Çπ1,80,000 GST

Used for: 70% taxable sales + 30% exempt sales

ITC to claim: ‚Çπ1,80,000 √ó 70% = ‚Çπ1,26,000

ITC to reverse: ‚Çπ1,80,000 √ó 30% = ‚Çπ54,000

3. Goods/Services for Personal Use

If business goods are used personally, reverse the proportionate ITC.

4. Switching from Regular to Composition Scheme

When moving to composition scheme, reverse ITC on stock, semi-finished goods, capital goods.

GSTR-2B vs GSTR-2A - Understanding the Difference

GSTR-2A (Auto-populated Statement)

  • Dynamic statement - updates as suppliers file returns
  • Shows purchases your suppliers have reported
  • Not a return - just informational

GSTR-2B (Static Statement)

  • Generated on 14th of next month
  • Shows ITC available for that month (locked after generation)
  • Use this for claiming ITC in GSTR-3B
Best Practice: Always reconcile your purchase register with GSTR-2B before filing GSTR-3B. Don't claim ITC on invoices not appearing in GSTR-2B - it will be disallowed in scrutiny.

Common ITC Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå Mistake 1: Claiming ITC Without Matching

Problem: Claiming ITC on invoices not in GSTR-2B

Solution: Verify all invoices appear in GSTR-2B before claiming

‚ùå Mistake 2: Not Reversing Blocked Credits

Problem: Claiming ITC on motor vehicles, food & beverages

Solution: Review blocked credits list before claiming

‚ùå Mistake 3: Missing Time Limit

Problem: Trying to claim ITC after September cutoff

Solution: Claim all eligible ITC before October 20 of next FY

‚ùå Mistake 4: Not Verifying Supplier Compliance

Problem: Supplier hasn't paid tax or filed returns

Solution: Verify GSTIN and return filing status regularly

‚ùå Mistake 5: Claiming on Credit Notes

Problem: Not reversing ITC when receiving credit notes

Solution: Reverse proportionate ITC when goods returned

Maximizing Your ITC Legally

‚úì Tip 1: Always Get Proper Invoices

Ensure every purchase has a valid GST invoice with correct GSTIN, invoice number, item details, and tax amount.

‚úì Tip 2: Reconcile Monthly

Match your purchase register with GSTR-2B every month. Don't wait for year-end.

‚úì Tip 3: Verify New Vendors

Before placing orders, verify vendor GSTIN and their return filing status using our GSTIN Verification tool.

‚úì Tip 4: Maintain Digital Records

Keep digital copies of all invoices, payments, and delivery challans for at least 6 years.

‚úì Tip 5: Pay Suppliers on Time

Pay within 180 days to avoid ITC reversal hassle.

‚úì Tip 6: Claim Capital Goods ITC Wisely

For capital goods, claim full ITC in the month of receipt (no need to spread over years).

ITC for Different Business Types

Manufacturers

Can claim ITC on raw materials, capital goods, factory rent, utilities, consumables.

Traders/Retailers

Can claim ITC on stock purchases, shop rent, packing materials, freight.

Service Providers

Can claim ITC on office rent, utilities, professional services, software subscriptions.

E-commerce Sellers

Can claim ITC on product purchases, packing, shipping, marketplace fees, advertising.

ITC and Composition Scheme

Bad news: Composition scheme dealers cannot claim any ITC. This is the biggest trade-off of the scheme.

  • Composition dealers pay flat 1-5% GST
  • But cannot claim ITC on purchases
  • Suitable for businesses with low input costs or retail businesses

Conclusion

Input Tax Credit is the backbone of GST - it eliminates cascading taxes and reduces your tax burden significantly. To maximize ITC:

  • Ensure all conditions are met before claiming
  • Reconcile purchases with GSTR-2B monthly
  • Verify supplier compliance regularly
  • Avoid blocked credits
  • Claim within time limits
  • Maintain proper documentation
