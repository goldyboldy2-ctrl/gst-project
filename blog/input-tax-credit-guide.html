Input Tax Credit (ITC) Explained - Complete Guide

Quick Summary: Input Tax Credit allows you to reduce GST liability by claiming credit for tax paid on purchases. Essential for reducing tax burden and improving cash flow.

What is Input Tax Credit?

Input Tax Credit (ITC) is the tax you've paid on purchases that can be used to reduce the tax you owe on sales. It's the cornerstone of GST that eliminates the cascading effect of taxes.

Conditions for Claiming ITC

1. You Must Have Tax Invoice

Valid tax invoice with all mandatory details including GSTIN, HSN code, and tax breakup.

2. Goods/Services Received

You must have actually received the goods or services (except for invoices under reverse charge).

3. Tax Paid by Supplier

Supplier must have deposited the tax to government. Check GSTR-2B for confirmation.

4. Supplier Filed GSTR-1

Invoice must appear in your GSTR-2B. If supplier hasn't filed, you can't claim ITC.

5. Your Return Filed

You must file GSTR-3B to claim ITC. Claim by November 30 or date of filing annual return.

6. Used for Business

Goods/services must be used for business purposes, not personal use.

ITC Reversal - When Required

In certain situations, you must reverse (pay back) the ITC claimed:

Common Reversal Scenarios:

  • Non-payment to Supplier: If you don't pay supplier within 180 days, reverse ITC on due date of GSTR-3B of month following 180 days
  • Personal Use: Goods/services used for personal purposes - reverse proportionate ITC
  • Exempt Supplies: If making both taxable and exempt supplies, reverse ITC on exempt portion
  • Lost/Destroyed Goods: Goods stolen, lost, destroyed, written off - reverse full ITC
  • Free Samples: Goods given as free samples - reverse ITC

How to Claim ITC (Step-by-Step)

1

Download GSTR-2B

Login to GST portal, download GSTR-2B for the month. This shows ITC available as per supplier's GSTR-1.

2

Reconcile with Your Records

Match GSTR-2B with your purchase register. Identify missing invoices and contact suppliers.

3

Claim in GSTR-3B Table 4

Report ITC in Table 4 of GSTR-3B. System auto-populates from GSTR-2B, verify and adjust if needed.

4

Set-off Against Liability

ITC automatically adjusts against output tax in Table 5. Pay only the balance amount.

Common ITC Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå Claiming Before Payment

Don't forget to reverse ITC if you don't pay supplier within 180 days.

‚ùå Not Matching with GSTR-2B

Always reconcile with GSTR-2B. Only claim what appears there.

‚ùå Claiming Blocked Credits

Know the Section 17(5) blocked credits. Don't claim ITC on motor vehicles, food, personal use.

‚ùå Wrong GSTIN on Invoice

Verify supplier GSTIN before accepting invoice. Wrong GSTIN = no ITC.

‚ùå Missing Time Limit

Claim ITC by November 30 of next financial year or date of annual return filing, whichever is earlier.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I claim ITC after 1 year?

A: No. ITC must be claimed by November 30 of next financial year or date of filing annual return, whichever is earlier. After that, ITC lapses.

Q: What if supplier cancelled their GSTIN?

A: You must reverse ITC claimed on invoices from cancelled GSTIN. Add interest as applicable.

Q: Can I claim ITC on demo products given to customers?

A: No. Demo products, free samples, and gifts are not eligible for ITC. You must reverse any ITC claimed.
