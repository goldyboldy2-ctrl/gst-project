Quick Summary

Input Tax Credit (ITC) lets you reduce the GST you owe by claiming credit for GST you've already paid on business purchases. It's GST's way of avoiding double taxation. This guide covers everything from basics to claiming rules.

What Exactly is Input Tax Credit?

Input Tax Credit (ITC) is the cornerstone of the GST system. In simple terms: ITC lets you subtract the GST you paid on purchases from the GST you collect on sales.

Think of it this way ‚Äî when you buy raw materials or services for your business, you pay GST to your supplier. When you sell your product or service, you collect GST from your customer. ITC allows you to claim back the GST you paid, so you only pay tax on the value you added.

Real-Life Example

You're a furniture maker:
‚Ä¢ You buy wood for ‚Çπ10,000 + ‚Çπ1,800 GST (18%) = ‚Çπ11,800 total
‚Ä¢ You make a table and sell it for ‚Çπ20,000 + ‚Çπ3,600 GST (18%) = ‚Çπ23,600 total
‚Ä¢ Without ITC: You pay ‚Çπ3,600 to the government
‚Ä¢ With ITC: You pay ‚Çπ3,600 - ‚Çπ1,800 = ‚Çπ1,800 only

You claimed ‚Çπ1,800 as Input Tax Credit and saved that amount!

Why Does ITC Matter?

ITC is crucial because:

  • Reduces your tax burden significantly
  • Prevents cascading effect (tax on tax)
  • Improves cash flow for your business
  • Makes your products more competitive in pricing
  • Ensures you only pay tax on value addition

Conditions to Claim Input Tax Credit

You cannot claim ITC on every purchase. The GST law has specific conditions that must be met:

1

You Must Have a Tax Invoice

The supplier must give you a proper GST invoice showing their GSTIN, your GSTIN, HSN code, and GST amount separately. No invoice = No ITC.

2

Goods/Services Must Be Received

You can only claim ITC after you've actually received the goods or services. Pre-payment ITC is allowed only after receipt.

3

Supplier Must Have Filed Their GST Return

Your supplier must upload the invoice in their GSTR-1. If they don't file, your ITC claim can be rejected. This is auto-verified in the GST portal.

4

You Must Have Paid the Supplier

If you don't pay your supplier within 180 days of the invoice date, the ITC gets reversed. Payment must be made to claim and retain ITC.

5

Used for Business Purposes Only

ITC is only available on purchases made for business use. Personal expenses, even if GST is charged, cannot be claimed as ITC.

6

You Must File GST Returns

To claim ITC, you must file your GSTR-3B return on time. Late filing can delay or deny your ITC claims.

How to Claim Input Tax Credit

The ITC claiming process is mostly automated through the GST portal:

Step 1: Verify Your Purchase Invoices

Log in to the GST portal ‚Üí Go to GSTR-2B (auto-populated from supplier's GSTR-1). This shows all invoices where you can claim ITC.

Step 2: Match with Your Books

Cross-check GSTR-2B invoices with your actual purchase records. Ensure all invoices are genuine and goods/services are received.

Step 3: File GSTR-3B

In your monthly GSTR-3B return, claim ITC in Table 4. The system auto-fills eligible ITC from GSTR-2B, but you can manually adjust if needed.

Step 4: Utilize ITC

Once claimed, ITC sits in your Electronic Credit Ledger. You can use it to pay your GST liability when filing returns. CGST credit pays CGST, SGST pays SGST, IGST can pay any.

When You Must Reverse ITC

Sometimes, ITC that was claimed earlier needs to be reversed (paid back). Common scenarios:

üîÑ

Reversal Scenarios

1. Non-payment to Supplier: If you don't pay within 180 days, reverse the ITC + 18% interest
2. Goods Used for Personal Use: If business goods are taken for personal use, reverse ITC proportionately
3. Supplier Cancels Invoice: If supplier issues credit note or cancels invoice, reverse ITC immediately
4. Goods Lost/Stolen/Destroyed: ITC must be reversed if goods are lost, stolen, or destroyed (except by natural calamity)
5. Exempt Supplies: If you make both taxable and exempt supplies, reverse ITC proportionate to exempt supplies

5 Common ITC Mistakes to Avoid

1

Claiming ITC on Personal Expenses

What happens: Business owners claim ITC on personal car, personal phone bills, family dinners labeled as "business meals."

Why it's wrong: ITC is strictly for business use only. Personal expenses are blocked credits under Section 17(5).

üí∞ Penalty: 100% ITC reversal + 18% interest + possible prosecution

‚úÖ How to Fix

Keep business and personal expenses completely separate. Use a dedicated business bank account and only claim ITC on genuine business purchases.

2

Not Checking if Supplier Filed Returns

What happens: You claim ITC based on invoices, but supplier never uploaded them in GSTR-1 or didn't file returns.

Why it's wrong: ITC is auto-matched with supplier's GSTR-1. If supplier doesn't file, your ITC claim gets rejected in reconciliation.

‚ö†Ô∏è Risk: ITC disallowed + interest on tax shortfall

‚úÖ How to Fix

Always check GSTR-2B before claiming ITC. Follow up with suppliers who haven't filed. Consider advance payment clauses that require GST filing proof.

3

Claiming ITC Before Receiving Goods

What happens: Business pays advance and immediately claims ITC, but goods arrive 3 months later.

Why it's wrong: ITC can only be claimed after actual receipt of goods/services. Advance payment ITC is allowed only after receipt.

‚ö†Ô∏è Risk: ITC reversal + interest

‚úÖ How to Fix

Maintain a proper goods receipt register. Claim ITC only in the month when goods/services are actually received, not when invoice is issued.

4

Wrong Utilization of ITC

What happens: Trying to use CGST credit to pay SGST, or vice versa.

Why it's wrong: ITC utilization has strict rules: CGST credit ‚Üí CGST/IGST only, SGST credit ‚Üí SGST/IGST only, IGST credit ‚Üí Any (IGST/CGST/SGST).

‚ö†Ô∏è Risk: Return rejection + manual correction needed

‚úÖ How to Fix

Understand the utilization order: First use IGST credit (most flexible), then use CGST/SGST as applicable. The portal usually auto-applies correctly.

5

Not Reversing ITC on Time

What happens: Claimed ITC but didn't pay supplier within 180 days. Forgot to reverse the ITC.

Why it's wrong: Non-payment reversal is mandatory. If you don't reverse voluntarily, department will demand reversal + interest + penalty.

üí∞ Penalty: ITC reversal + 18% interest from date of claim

‚úÖ How to Fix

Maintain a payment tracker for all purchases. Set calendar reminders for 150 days from invoice date. Reverse ITC in next GSTR-3B if payment not made by 180 days.

ITC Reconciliation ‚Äî Why It Matters

ITC reconciliation is matching your claimed ITC with what suppliers have reported. This prevents mismatches and notices.

üîç

How to Reconcile ITC

Step 1: Download GSTR-2B from portal (shows supplier-uploaded invoices)
Step 2: Match with your purchase register and accounting books
Step 3: Identify mismatches ‚Äî missing invoices, wrong amounts, duplicates
Step 4: Contact suppliers to correct their GSTR-1
Step 5: Claim only matched ITC in GSTR-3B

Do this monthly ‚Äî don't wait for year-end. Early reconciliation prevents big surprises during audits.

Frequently Asked Questions

‚ùì

Can I claim ITC if I lost the invoice?

No. Invoice is mandatory. However, if supplier filed GSTR-1 and you have proof of payment, you can request a duplicate invoice from supplier.

‚ùì

What if supplier cancels their GST registration?

If supplier's registration is cancelled, all ITC claimed from them after cancellation date must be reversed immediately. Check supplier GSTIN status regularly.

Can I claim ITC for previous years?

ITC can be claimed up to 30th November of next financial year or date of filing annual return (GSTR-9), whichever is earlier. After that, ITC lapses.

What is GSTR-2B and how is it different from GSTR-2A?

GSTR-2B is a static, auto-generated statement available after GSTR-1 filing deadline. GSTR-2A was dynamic (real-time). Use GSTR-2B for claiming ITC ‚Äî it's the final, locked version.
