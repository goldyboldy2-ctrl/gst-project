How to File GST Return Online in India 2026

Published: February 8, 2026 ‚Ä¢ 10 min read
üí°

Quick Summary: Filing GST returns online is mandatory for all registered taxpayers. This guide covers step-by-step process for filing GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B, documents needed, deadlines, and common mistakes to avoid.

What is GST Return?

A GST return is a document containing details of all your income (sales), purchases, tax collected on sales (output tax), and tax paid on purchases (input tax). Every registered taxpayer must file GST returns with the government.

Think of it as a summary report you submit to the government showing how much tax you collected from customers, how much tax you paid on purchases, and the difference (which you either pay or claim as refund).

Types of GST Returns

GSTR-1 (Outward Supplies)

Contains details of all sales/outward supplies made during the month.

Due Date: 11th of next month

Filed By: Regular taxpayers

GSTR-3B (Monthly Summary Return)

Summary return showing total sales, purchases, and tax payable for the month.

Due Date: 20th of next month

Filed By: All regular taxpayers (most common)

GSTR-9 (Annual Return)

Consolidated annual return summarizing all monthly returns for the financial year.

Due Date: 31st December of next financial year

Filed By: Regular taxpayers (turnover > ‚Çπ2 crore)

GSTR-4 (Composition Scheme)

Quarterly return for businesses under composition scheme.

Due Date: 18th of month after quarter end

Filed By: Composition scheme taxpayers

Documents Required for GST Return Filing

Documents You'll Need:

  • Sales Invoices: All tax invoices issued during the month
  • Purchase Invoices: Invoices received from suppliers
  • Debit/Credit Notes: For amendments or returns
  • E-way Bills: For goods transportation (if applicable)
  • Bank Statements: For payment verification
  • Previous Returns: To check carried forward ITC
  • GSTIN Credentials: Username and password for GST portal

How to File GSTR-3B (Step-by-Step)

GSTR-3B is the most commonly filed return. Here's the complete step-by-step process:

1

Login to GST Portal

Visit www.gst.gov.in and login using your GSTIN, username, and password. Complete two-factor authentication.

2

Navigate to Returns Dashboard

Go to Services ‚Üí Returns ‚Üí Returns Dashboard. Select the financial year and return filing period (month/quarter).

3

Click on GSTR-3B

Click on "PREPARE ONLINE" button next to GSTR-3B for the relevant period.

4

Fill Table 3.1 - Outward Supplies

Enter details of your sales:

  • Taxable value of sales
  • Integrated Tax (IGST), Central Tax (CGST), State Tax (SGST)
  • Separate rows for different tax rates (5%, 12%, 18%, 28%)
5

Fill Table 4 - Input Tax Credit (ITC)

Enter ITC available from purchases. System auto-populates from GSTR-2B. Verify and make corrections if needed.

6

Check Table 5 - Tax Payable

System automatically calculates: Tax on Sales - ITC = Tax Payable. Verify the amount.

7

Pay Tax (If Applicable)

If tax is payable, go to Electronic Cash Ledger and make payment via net banking, debit card, or NEFT/RTGS.

8

File Return with DSC/EVC

Click "FILE GSTR-3B WITH DSC" or "FILE GSTR-3B WITH EVC" to submit. Verify using Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) or Electronic Verification Code (EVC).

Success!

Once filed successfully, you'll receive an Application Reference Number (ARN). Download the acknowledgment for your records.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå Missing the Deadline

Late filing attracts ‚Çπ50/day penalty (‚Çπ20 for nil returns). Set calendar reminders 3 days before due date.

‚ùå Incorrect GSTIN

Always verify supplier GSTIN before claiming ITC. Wrong GSTIN = ITC rejection.

‚ùå Claiming Ineligible ITC

ITC not available on personal expenses, health services, food & beverages. Know the blocked credits.

‚ùå Not Reconciling GSTR-2B

Always match your purchase records with GSTR-2B before filing. Mismatches cause ITC denial.

‚ùå Wrong Tax Rate Application

Use correct HSN codes and tax rates. Verify GST rate before invoicing to avoid corrections later.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I revise a filed GST return?

A: No, GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B cannot be revised once filed. However, you can make amendments in the next month's return. For significant errors, consult a CA.

Q: What if my supplier hasn't filed GSTR-1?

A: You cannot claim ITC if your supplier hasn't filed GSTR-1. The invoice won't appear in your GSTR-2B. Contact your supplier immediately or risk losing ITC.

Q: Do I need to file if I have no sales?

A: Yes, nil returns are mandatory even with zero business activity. Penalty of ‚Çπ20/day applies for late nil return filing.

Q: Can I file returns offline?

A: No, GST returns must be filed online through the GST portal. However, you can prepare returns offline using tools and upload them to the portal.
